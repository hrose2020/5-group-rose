Technology in Youth

Technology: the branch of knowledge that deals with the creation and use of technical means and their interrelation with life, society, and the environment, drawing upon such subjects as industrial arts, engineering, applied science, and pure science.

What is ICT?

Information and communications technology (ICT): All the technology used to handle telecommunications, broadcast media, intelligent building management systems, audiovisual processing and transmission systems, and network-based control and monitoring functions.

Background Information

  • The past 15 years have been a pivotal time for technological advances, with cell phones and social media completely changing ways of communication.
  • Communication with family, friends, or strangers now is less face to face and more through a screen.
  • Technology has made it much easier to get in touch with friends or anyone in the world in seconds, via text, facetime, or social media.
  • While there have been fluctuations, there is not a clear upward or downward tendency in the percent of teens reporting daily face-to-face contact with their friends

The Facts

  • 72% of all teens spend time with friends via social media; 23% do so daily.
  • 79% of all teens instant message their friends; 27% do so daily.
  • 57% of teens have met a new friend online, but only 20% of all teens have met an online friend in person.
  • 88% of teen social media users believe people share too much information about themselves on social media.
  • 54% of teens contact their friends on a daily basis using texting and 33% interact with them face to face.
  • 53% of social media-using teens have seen people posting to social media about events to which they were not invited.
